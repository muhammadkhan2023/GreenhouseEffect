Greenhouse Gases: Impacts on Humans and the Environment

What Are Greenhouse Gases?

Greenhouse gases, such as carbon dioxide (CO2), methane (CH4), and nitrous oxide (N2O), trap heat in the Earth's atmosphere. While they play a critical role in maintaining the planet's temperature, excessive levels lead to climate change and significant health and environmental impacts.

Effects on Human Health

  • Respiratory Problems: Pollutants like ozone and fine particulate matter contribute to asthma and other respiratory illnesses.
  • Heat Stress: Rising global temperatures increase cases of heat exhaustion and heat stroke.
  • Spread of Diseases: Climate change allows disease-carrying insects like mosquitoes to spread malaria and dengue to new regions.
  • Food and Water Insecurity: Changes in climate disrupt agricultural productivity and clean water availability.

Learn more about the health impacts of climate change from the World Health Organization.

Effects on the Environment

  • Rising Sea Levels: Melting ice caps and glaciers result in coastal flooding, endangering ecosystems and human settlements.
  • Loss of Biodiversity: Changing climates force species to migrate or face extinction.
  • Extreme Weather Events: More frequent hurricanes, droughts, and wildfires damage ecosystems and disrupt human lives.
  • Ocean Acidification: Absorption of CO2 by oceans affects marine life, including coral reefs and shellfish.

Explore NASA's data on climate change effects on the environment.

How You Can Help

Here are some ways you can reduce greenhouse gas emissions:

  • Adopt renewable energy sources like solar and wind.
  • Conserve energy at home by switching to energy-efficient appliances.
  • Support policies aimed at reducing carbon emissions.
